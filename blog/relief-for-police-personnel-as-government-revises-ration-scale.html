Relief for Police Personnel as Government Revises Ration Scale

The revision of the ration scale for police personnel has brought a wave of relief to those serving in the force. According to recent statistics, in the year 2020, the average expenditure on rations per police personnel was around $500 per month. However, with the rising cost of living and increasing food prices, this amount had become insufficient, leading to difficulties for many personnel in managing their daily expenses. As of 2022, the government has taken note of this issue and has revised the ration scale, providing a significant increase in the monthly allowance for police personnel.

Introduction to the Revised Ration Scale

The revised ration scale aims to provide a more realistic and sufficient allowance for police personnel, taking into account the current market prices of essential commodities. The new scale provides for an increase of up to 20% in the monthly ration allowance, which is expected to greatly benefit the personnel and their families. This move is seen as a positive step towards improving the overall well-being and morale of the police force.
