Understanding the Ration Scam

The CBI has alleged that the three ITBP officers, along with other accused, siphoned off ration supplies meant for the Dehradun battalion and sold them in the open market, causing a loss of Rs 70 lakh to the government. The scam is believed to have been carried out over several months, with the accused using fake bills and vouchers to cover their tracks. The CBI has registered a case under the Prevention of Corruption Act and is currently investigating the matter.

The ration scam has raised serious concerns about the integrity of the ration supply system and the accountability of those responsible for managing it. It has also highlighted the need for greater transparency and oversight in the procurement and distribution of ration supplies.
