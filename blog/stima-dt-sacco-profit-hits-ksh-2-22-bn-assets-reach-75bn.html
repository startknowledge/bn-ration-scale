Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about Stima DT Sacco and sacco societies in general:

What is a sacco society?
A sacco society is a type of cooperative financial institution that provides financial services to its members.
How do I join a sacco society?
To join a sacco society, you typically need to meet certain eligibility criteria, such as being a resident of a particular area or working for a particular employer. You will also need to pay a membership fee and deposit a certain amount of money into your account.
What types of financial products do sacco societies offer?
Sacco societies typically offer a range of financial products, including savings accounts, loans, investments, and insurance products.
How do sacco societies make money?
Sacco societies make money by earning interest on loans, investing in other businesses or assets, and providing other financial services to their members.
Are sacco societies regulated?
Yes, sacco societies are regulated by government agencies, such as the Sacco Societies Regulatory Authority (SASRA) in Kenya.
