Introduction to ACB and its Role in Combating Corruption

The Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB) is a specialized agency responsible for investigating and prosecuting corruption cases. The ACB has the power to register FIRs against individuals accused of corruption, conduct investigations, and prosecute cases in court. The ACB's primary objective is to prevent and combat corruption, ensuring that public officials and individuals in positions of power are held accountable for their actions.

In recent years, the ACB has been actively involved in registering FIRs against former government officials and public servants, including those who have been accused of corruption during their tenure. This has led to a significant increase in the number of corruption cases being investigated and prosecuted. For instance, in 2018, the ACB registered 300 FIRs against corrupt individuals, resulting in the recovery of over $10 million in ill-gotten gains (Source: ACB Annual Report 2018).

Understanding the Process of Registering an FIR with the ACB

The process of registering an FIR with the ACB involves several steps, including:

  • **Filing a complaint**: The complainant files a complaint with the ACB, providing detailed information about the alleged corruption.
  • **Verification of complaint**: The ACB verifies the complaint, conducting a preliminary investigation to determine whether there is sufficient evidence to register an FIR.
  • **Registration of FIR**: If the complaint is found to be genuine, the ACB registers an FIR against the accused individual.
  • **Investigation**: The ACB conducts a thorough investigation, gathering evidence and interviewing witnesses.
  • **Chargesheet**: The ACB files a chargesheet against the accused individual, outlining the charges and evidence.
