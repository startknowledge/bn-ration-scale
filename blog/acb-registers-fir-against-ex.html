Introduction to ACB and FIR Registration

The Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB) is a specialized agency responsible for investigating and prosecuting corruption cases. The ACB has the power to register FIRs against individuals, including ex-officials, who are suspected of corruption. The registration of an FIR is the first step in the investigation process, and it is essential to understand the implications of an FIR registration. In the year 2018, the ACB introduced a new framework for registering FIRs, which has streamlined the process and made it more efficient (Source: ACB Press Release 2018).
