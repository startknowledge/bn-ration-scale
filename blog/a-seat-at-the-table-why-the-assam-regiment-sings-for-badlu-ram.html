Badlu Ram was born in 1920 in Assam, a region with a rich military tradition. His early life was shaped by the British Raj’s colonial military system, where the Assam Regiment was already a highly respected force known for its bravery in World War I and II.

Common Mistakes in Leadership (And How to Avoid Them)

Even the best leaders make mistakes. Here are five pitfalls that destroy loyalty—and how to prevent them.

1. Micromanaging Instead of Empowering

Mistake: Controlling every detail, stifling creativity. Solution:

  • Delegate with trust – Give employees autonomy to make decisions.
  • Provide resources – If they need tools, equip them properly.

Example:

  • Amazon’s "Two-Pizza Rule" – If a team can’t be fed by two pizzas, it’s too large, meaning better delegation.

2. Ignoring Employee Feedback

Mistake: Assuming you know best, never listening to concerns. Solution:

  • Hold regular feedback sessions – Use anonymous surveys if needed.
  • Act on suggestions – Show that their voice matters.

Example:

  • Netflix’s "Freedom & Responsibility" culture encourages open feedback at all levels.

3. Favoritism and Nepotism

Mistake: Playing favorites, creating resentment. Solution:

  • Promote based on merit – Have clear, objective criteria.
  • Rotate leadership roles – Prevent long-term bias.

Example:

  • Google’s "Project Oxygen" found that the best managers are great coaches, not just favorites of the boss.

4. Overpromising and Underdelivering

Mistake: Making unrealistic promises, then falling short. Solution:

  • Set realistic expectations – Be honest about timelines and resources.
  • Communicate delays transparently – Bad news early is better than surprises.

Example:

  • Apple’s "Ship in Silence" culture ensures deliverables are met before public announcements.

5. Neglecting Work-Life Balance

Mistake: Expecting 24/7 availability, burning out the team. Solution:

  • Enforce boundaries – No emails after hours unless critical.
  • Encourage vacations – Mandate PTO to prevent burnout.

Example:

  • Patagonia’s "1% for the Planet" + 1% Time Off policy ensures employees take real breaks.
