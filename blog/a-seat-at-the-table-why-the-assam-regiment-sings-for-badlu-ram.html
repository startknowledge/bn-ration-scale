Chapter 1: The Legend of Badlu Ram – A Cultural Pillar of Assam

1.1 Who is Badlu Ram? The Divine Guardian of Assam

Badlu Ram, also known as Ramdhunja, is a folk deity deeply embedded in Assamese mythology. Unlike the Hindu god Ram, Badlu Ram is a localized, semi-divine figure associated with protection, prosperity, and the well-being of the land. According to legend, he was a saint-warrior who fought against demonic forces to safeguard Assam’s people and their way of life.

The most famous story surrounding Badlu Ram is his battle against the demon king Bhasmasur, where he emerged victorious, symbolizing justice and resilience. Over time, he evolved into a patron deity of farmers, soldiers, and the Assamese community, often invoked during harvest seasons, military campaigns, and personal crises.

1.2 The Evolution of Badlu Ram Worship in Assamese Society

The worship of Badlu Ram predates organized Hinduism in Assam, making it a unique indigenous tradition. Unlike mainstream Hindu deities, Badlu Ram’s cult is regional, oral, and deeply tied to local folklore. His temples, often small and unassuming, are found in villages, tea gardens, and even military camps of the Assam Regiment.

Historically, Assamese warriors (including those who later formed the Assam Regiment) would pray to Badlu Ram before battles, believing his blessings would ensure victory and protection. This practice persisted even after the regiment was officially established in 1941, blending military discipline with cultural devotion.

1.3 Why Does the Assam Regiment Hold Badlu Ram in Such High Regard?

The Assam Regiment’s connection to Badlu Ram is not just religious—it’s a matter of identity. Here’s why this bond is so strong:

✅ Cultural Continuity – The regiment was formed from Assamese tribes and communities who already revered Badlu Ram. Maintaining this tradition ensures roots are not forgotten in a modern military setting.

✅ Morale Booster – Soldiers believe that Badlu Ram’s blessings provide strength in combat and resilience in tough conditions.

✅ Unity Among Ranks – The puja ceremonies bring together officers, jawans, and support staff, fostering a sense of brotherhood.

✅ Distinctive Identity – Unlike other regiments that follow pan-Indian traditions, the Assam Regiment’s devotion to Badlu Ram sets it apart, making it uniquely Assamese.

✅ Legacy of Ancestors – Many soldiers trace their family lineages to Assamese warriors who once prayed to Badlu Ram before battles. This generational continuity strengthens their commitment.

This event is not just a religious ceremony—it’s a military parade, cultural showcase, and morale-boosting event.

Key Features of the Puja:

  • Military Drill & Procession – Soldiers march in uniforms adorned with Badlu Ram’s symbols, carrying traditional Assamese flags (Dhunghar).
  • Offerings & Prayers – A special puja thali is prepared with rice, fruits, flowers, and weapons (symbolizing protection).
  • Kirtan & Bhajans – Soldiers and civilians sing devotional songs in praise of Badlu Ram, often composed in Assamese and Bodo languages.
  • Community Participation – Local villagers, Assamese diaspora, and even other defense forces are invited, making it a unifying event.
  • Award Ceremonies – The regiment honors outstanding soldiers with Badlu Ram-themed awards, linking military excellence with divine blessings.
