Chapter 1: The Legend of Badlu Ram – A Song Steeped in History

1.1 The Origins: From Folklore to Military Anthem

The story of Badlu Ram is as old as the Assam Regiment itself, tracing its roots back to the 19th century when British colonial forces recruited locals from Assam to form the Assam Infantry. Over time, these regiments evolved into the Assam Regiment, one of India’s most decorated military units.

The song "Badlu Ram" is believed to have originated from Assamese folklore, where it was sung by laborers and farmers as a work song or a call to unity. The lyrics, though not officially documented in ancient texts, are said to have been passed down orally, evolving over generations.

The most popular version goes like this:

"Badlu Ram, Badlu Ram, Badlu Ram, Badlu Ram, Badlu Ram, Badlu Ram, Badlu Ram, Badlu Ram…"

While the exact meaning remains debated, scholars suggest it could be:

  • A call to unity among workers (similar to "Come all ye faithful").
  • A symbol of resilience—Badlu (meaning "change" or "transformation") and Ram (a revered name in Hindu culture) together represent adaptation and strength.
  • A military rallying cry, reinforcing discipline and brotherhood.

By the early 20th century, as the Assam Regiment grew in strength, soldiers began incorporating "Badlu Ram" into their pre-battle rituals, training sessions, and even casual conversations.
